Please read this important health and safety information before using Exercitron.

⚠️ Important Notice

Exercitron provides general fitness guidance only and does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before beginning any exercise program, particularly if you have any pre-existing health conditions.

1. Not Medical Advice

The workout plans, exercise recommendations, coaching feedback, and all other content provided by Exercitron are for informational and general fitness purposes only. This information is not intended to be a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Never disregard professional medical advice or delay in seeking it because of something you have read or received from Exercitron. If you think you may have a medical emergency, call your doctor or emergency services immediately.

2. Consult Your Physician

Before beginning any new exercise program, we strongly recommend consulting with your physician or a qualified healthcare provider, especially if you:

  • Have any heart condition or cardiovascular disease
  • Have high blood pressure or take blood pressure medication
  • Have diabetes or blood sugar regulation issues
  • Have any bone, joint, or muscle injuries
  • Are pregnant or postpartum
  • Are over 40 years old and have been sedentary
  • Have any other chronic health condition
  • Answered YES to any PAR-Q question in the app

3. PAR-Q — Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire

Exercitron uses the PAR-Q© (Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire), a widely accepted health screening tool developed by the Canadian Society for Exercise Physiology. The seven PAR-Q questions are:

# Question If YES
1 Has your doctor ever said that you have a heart condition and that you should only do physical activity recommended by a doctor? Consult your doctor before any exercise
2 Do you feel pain in your chest when you do physical activity? Seek medical evaluation immediately
3 In the past month, have you had chest pain when you were not doing physical activity? See your doctor before starting exercise
4 Do you lose your balance because of dizziness, or do you ever lose consciousness? Consult your doctor first
5 Do you have a bone or joint problem that could be made worse by a change in your physical activity? Get medical clearance first
6 Is your doctor currently prescribing drugs for your blood pressure or heart condition? Exercise under medical supervision
7 Do you know of any other reason why you should not do physical activity? Address that reason with your doctor first

PAR-Q© 2002. Used with permission from the Canadian Society for Exercise Physiology.

6. Progressive Overload Coaching

Exercitron's progressive overload coaching feature analyzes your workout history to suggest weight increases, decreases, deload weeks, and other training modifications. These suggestions are algorithmic and general in nature — they are not personalized medical advice.

Always use your own judgment. If a suggested weight increase causes pain or discomfort, do not proceed. Listen to your body above all else.

🚨 Stop Exercise Immediately If You Experience:

  • Chest pain, pressure, tightness, or discomfort
  • Severe shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting
  • Unusual or severe joint or muscle pain
  • Heart palpitations or irregular heartbeat
  • Nausea or vomiting during exercise
  • Sudden severe headache

If you experience any of these symptoms, stop exercising immediately and seek medical attention. Call 911 (US) or your local emergency number if symptoms are severe.

Always apply common sense and your own knowledge of your body when following any AI-generated fitness recommendation.
